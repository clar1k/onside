ONSIDE — JUDGE Q&A

Why not use an existing prediction market?
Onside is sports-native: TxODDS match data arrives with a Merkle proof, and the Solana program verifies that proof directly. Settlement does not depend on an operator or a propose/challenge/vote process.

Why does this need blockchain?
Users should not have to trust a sportsbook database with custody, settlement, or payouts. Solana makes the market state, proof verification, and payout receipts public and independently verifiable.

Is Onside parimutuel?
Yes. Each YES/NO market uses a shared pool, so payouts come from the losing side rather than fixed house odds. Seed liquidity makes new pools usable before organic bettors arrive.

Who runs the keeper?
Anyone can submit the signed result proof. The keeper automates submission, but it cannot select or alter the winning outcome.

What can you demonstrate without a live World Cup match?
Use the recorded devnet replay: connect a wallet, inspect the custom markets and pools, place a YES/NO combo, then show the on-chain program and proof-based settlement flow.

What is already built?
A deployed Anchor program on Solana devnet, a working frontend, parimutuel YES/NO pools, combo betting, market creation and liquidity seeding, and automated proof submission.
