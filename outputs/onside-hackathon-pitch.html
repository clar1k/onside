Onside is a trustless football prediction market built on Solana. Fans can bet on match outcomes, goals, cards, corners, and same-match combinations—but no administrator gets to decide the winner. Every result is verified using cryptographic data from TxODDS.
The problem

The last step still asks:
who decides the result?

Propose→Challenge→Vote
Settlement becomes a human interpretation layer—even when trading itself is on-chain.
Source: Polymarket resolution documentation02
Prediction markets promise transparency, but settlement often introduces interpretation again. A proposer declares an outcome, others may challenge it, and disputed cases can end in a vote. Users can inspect the final transaction, but they still depend on people agreeing about the result.
The product

Onside gives fans a familiar sportsbook experience. They connect a wallet and choose YES or NO across markets. We also built same-match parlays: users can combine multiple outcomes into one ticket, with the parlay paid only when every selection wins. Individual markets use shared parimutuel pools, so users do not need to find a direct counterparty. Their stake joins the pool, and winners share the payout.

The keeper submits proof. It never chooses the winner.
Source: TxODDS TxLINE04
When the match finishes, TxODDS supplies the final score and Merkle proof. The keeper submits that proof to Onside. The Onside program calls the TxODDS oracle program, verifies the value against the on-chain root, and derives the winner from the market predicate. The keeper can submit proof, but it cannot invent a score or select an outcome.

It turns TxODDS verified sports data into a prediction market that fans can understand and anyone can audit. Onside: the result is proven, not trusted.
