RFQ: Request for Quote

Like asking several currency-exchange desks what they will give you for one exact transaction. Each returns a firm, time-limited offer; you choose whether to accept.

Exchange analogy

“Exchange my $100,000 into EUR” → desks offer EUR amounts → accept the best quote → currencies change hands.

Onside: “Price my 10 USDC combo” → makers offer payouts → accept the best quote → the combo bet is created.

Lifecycle

User request → maker quote → user acceptance → collateral locked/trade executed → settlement

ObjectMeaning
Request“Price this exact combo and size.” Not a trade.
QuoteSigned offer containing price/payout, size, expiry, and nonce.
AcceptanceUser agrees before expiry; this creates the trade.
CollateralFunds or inventory securing the maximum promised payout.
SettlementOnside proves every leg and pays according to the accepted contract.
Remember: RFQ is a price-discovery and counterparty-selection mechanism. It does not create liquidity. The quoting maker or vault supplies the economic backing.
